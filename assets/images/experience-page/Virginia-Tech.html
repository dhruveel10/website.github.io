Virginia Tech introduces new academic logo

Published: Sep. 26, 2017 at 11:45 AM EDT

Virginia Tech is starting a new chapter with the unveiling of a new academic logo.

The new brand is the culmination of more than a year’s worth of research that included feedback from Virginia Tech.

The brand was finalized and presented to the Board of Visitors at its September 2017 meeting.

"With strong institutional and community support to create transdisciplinary research programs and build mutually beneficial partnerships and networks, Virginia Tech is well-positioned to lead breakthrough change,” said Theresa Mayer, vice president for research and innovation, "The new brand lays the foundation to accurately communicate that vision and empowers us to make it a reality."

The University says the cost to implement the new logo over the next three years is one million dollars. That will go towards replacing things like signs. The cost to develop the new logo was roughly 25 thousand dollars.

In a news release, Virginia Tech says they are mirroring the shape of the “flying VT” proudly displayed by Hokie student-athletes.

The Chicago maroon and burnt orange, the HokieBird, the university seal, and Hokie Stone, will not change.

The former academic logo, a shield depicting the words "Virginia Tech," which has served the university since 1991, will be retired and added to the collection of vintage marks in the marketing vault.

The tagline, "Invent the Future," which was added in 2006, will also be retired.

A tagline will no longer be part of the university mark.
